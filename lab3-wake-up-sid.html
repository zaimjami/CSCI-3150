Overview

Wake Up Sid is a 2009 Indian Hindi-language coming-of-age comedy-drama written and directed by Ayan Mukerji in his directorial debut and produced by Karan Johar under Dharma Productions. The film stars Ranbir Kapoor and Konkona Sen Sharma, with supporting performances by Anupam Kher, Supriya Pathak, Namit Das, and Rahul Khanna. Set in contemporary Mumbai, it follows Siddharth “Sid” Mehra, a carefree college student who matures through friendship with Aisha Banerjee, an aspiring writer from Kolkata.

Released theatrically on 2 October 2009, the film received widespread acclaim for its direction, contemporary themes, performances, and music, becoming a commercial success. Over time, it has been regarded as a defining youth-focused film of its decade for its relatable urban characters and gentle, believable transformation arc.

Plot

Siddharth “Sid” Mehra is a bright but aimless student in Mumbai whose comfortable lifestyle keeps him unmotivated. He meets Aisha Banerjee, an aspiring writer from Kolkata, who has moved to the city to work at Mumbai Beat magazine. After failing his final exams and clashing with his parents and friends, Sid moves into Aisha’s newly rented apartment and begins to shoulder everyday responsibilities.

Encouraged by Aisha, Sid rekindles a love for photography and earns an internship at Mumbai Beat. As he learns discipline and independence, he reconciles with his family and friends. Aisha briefly dates her editor, Kabir, but realizes they’re incompatible and discovers her feelings for Sid. After professional and personal milestones, Sid and Aisha finally confess their love, marking Sid’s transformation from a carefree teen into a grounded young adult.

Production

The film was shot extensively in Mumbai—including South Mumbai and Bandra—with college sequences filmed at H. R. College of Commerce and Economics near Churchgate. The story reflects director Ayan Mukerji’s contemporary, character-focused sensibility, framing urban independence and found-family experiences common to young professionals in big cities.

Release

Wake Up Sid opened on 2 October 2009 and performed well in India and overseas. It later aired on Indian television networks and was released on home media. The film’s gentle tone, music, and lead performances helped it find a long tail of audience appreciation beyond its theatrical run.

Critical Reception

Critics widely praised the film’s naturalistic direction, performances, and believable urban themes. Reviews highlighted Ranbir Kapoor’s restrained lead turn and Konkona Sen Sharma’s grounded portrayal of an independent writer. Some dissenters found the ending conventional, but overall consensus remains strongly positive.

Box Office

With an estimated budget around ₹18 crore, Wake Up Sid grossed over ₹47 crore worldwide. It opened strongly in India, maintained momentum across multiple weeks, and performed respectably in overseas markets.

Accolades

At the 55th Filmfare Awards, the film earned nine nominations and won three: Best Debut Director (Ayan Mukerji), Best Actor (Critics) (Ranbir Kapoor), and Best Female Playback Singer (Kavita Seth for “Iktara”). It also received honors at Producers Guild and IIFA awards, among others.

Soundtrack

The songs were composed by Shankar–Ehsaan–Loy with lyrics by Javed Akhtar, and the background score by Amit Trivedi. The track “Iktara”—associated with Trivedi and vocalist Kavita Seth—became emblematic of the film’s mellow, introspective tone.
