Overview

Gehraiyaan (transl. Depths) is a 2022 Indian Hindi-language romantic drama film directed by Shakun Batra and produced by Karan Johar, Apoorva Mehta, and Somen Mishra under Dharma Productions in collaboration with Viacom18 Studios and Jouska Films. The film stars Deepika Padukone and Siddhant Chaturvedi in lead roles, alongside Ananya Panday and Dhairya Karwa, with supporting performances from Rajat Kapoor and Naseeruddin Shah. Set against a contemporary urban backdrop, the narrative explores themes of infidelity, trauma, and emotional complexity through the intertwined lives of four individuals navigating love, ambition, and betrayal.

Principal photography took place in Goa, Mumbai, and Alibag, with cinematography by Kaushal Shah. The screenplay was co-written by Batra, Ayesha Devitre Dhillon, Sumit Roy, and Yash Sahai. The soundtrack features original compositions by OAFF and Savera, with lyrics by Kausar Munir and Ankur Tewari.

The film premiered digitally on 11 February 2022 on Amazon Prime Video, receiving mixed-to-positive reviews. Performances — particularly Padukone’s — alongside cinematography and soundtrack were widely praised, while the screenplay and third act drew criticism.

Plot

Alisha “Al” Khanna, a 30-year-old yoga instructor in Mumbai, supports her unmotivated boyfriend Karan while struggling with anxiety and trauma from her mother’s suicide. Estranged from her father Vinod, she reconnects with her cousin Tia and Tia’s fiancé Zain Oberoi, a real-estate developer. Alisha and Zain begin a secret affair, intensifying as Alisha’s frustrations with Karan grow.

Zain manipulates both women while his company faces investigation for fraud. Alisha becomes pregnant, while Tia unknowingly pledges her family’s property to save Zain’s firm. Zain sells it secretly, and tensions spiral. In a confrontation on his yacht, Zain dies accidentally. Alisha miscarries and is later blackmailed by Zain’s associate Jitesh.

Tia later reveals Alisha’s true parentage: she is the daughter of Tia’s father Kumud and Sonali. This revelation reshapes Alisha’s estrangement from Vinod. Two years later, she attends Karan’s engagement, reconnects with Tia, and looks toward a more peaceful life. The film ends on a cliffhanger with a mysterious reunion.

Production

Development and Casting

Originally, Shakun Batra was to direct a biopic on Osho but shifted to Gehraiyaan. Deepika Padukone joined in December 2019, with Siddhant Chaturvedi and Ananya Panday cast soon after. Dhairya Karwa was later added as the love interest of Padukone’s character.

Filming

Filming was initially planned in Sri Lanka but moved to Goa due to COVID-19 travel restrictions. Production began in September 2020, resumed in Mumbai later that year, and concluded in August 2021. The film was notable for employing an intimacy department for choreographing sensitive scenes.

Soundtrack

The music was composed by OAFF and Savera, marking their mainstream debut. Lyrics were written by Kausar Munir and Ankur Tewari. The Hindi version of “Frontline,” performed by Lothika Jha, was adapted as part of the soundtrack.

Reception

Gehraiyaan received mixed reviews. Performances, cinematography, and soundtrack were praised, while the screenplay and climax were criticized. Rotten Tomatoes reported a 64% approval rating based on 22 reviews.

Positive reviews highlighted Padukone’s performance and the film’s technical strengths. Critics such as Anna M. M. Vetticad of Firstpost rated it highly, while others like Shubhra Gupta of The Indian Express found it lacking in depth.
