Gehraiyaan (transl. Depths) is a 2022 Indian Hindi-language romantic drama film directed by Shakun Batra and produced by Karan Johar, Apoorva Mehta, and Somen Mishra under Dharma Productions, in collaboration with Viacom18 Studios and Jouska Films. The film stars Deepika Padukone and Siddhant Chaturvedi in lead roles, alongside Ananya Panday and Dhairya Karwa, with supporting performances from Rajat Kapoor and Naseeruddin Shah.

Plot

Alisha "Al" Khanna, a 30-year-old yoga instructor in Mumbai, is running a Yoga studio and developing a wellness app. She supports her boyfriend Karan, an aspiring but unmotivated writer. Haunted by her mother’s suicide and estranged from her father, Alisha feels unfulfilled.

During a trip to her cousin Tia’s beach house, Alisha meets Tia’s fiancé Zain Oberoi, a wealthy real estate developer. Despite being engaged, Zain flirts with Alisha, and they begin an affair. Their secret relationship intensifies, while Alisha’s frustrations with Karan grow.

As Zain’s business faces financial trouble, he manipulates Tia into pledging her family property. Alisha later discovers she is pregnant. Zain’s deceit unravels, leading to a confrontation on his yacht where he accidentally falls, hits his head, and drowns. Alisha returns home, suffers a miscarriage, and is later blackmailed by Zain’s associate Jitesh.

Eventually, Alisha learns shocking truths about her parentage: she is the product of her mother’s affair with Tia’s father. Reconnecting with her cousin years later, Alisha looks toward a more hopeful future despite the past’s traumas.

Production

Principal photography took place in Goa, Mumbai, and Alibag, with cinematography by Kaushal Shah. The screenplay was co-written by Batra, Ayesha Devitre Dhillon, Sumit Roy, and Yash Sahai. The soundtrack features compositions by OAFF and Savera, with lyrics by Kausar Munir and Ankur Tewari.

Gehraiyaan premiered digitally on 11 February 2022 on Amazon Prime Video, receiving mixed-to-positive reviews. While performances and cinematography were praised, critics noted weaknesses in the screenplay and third act.
