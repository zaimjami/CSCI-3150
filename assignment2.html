Overview

Zindagi Na Milegi Dobara (transl. “You only live once”) is a 2011 Indian Hindi-language road comedy-drama film directed by Zoya Akhtar and produced by Farhan Akhtar and Ritesh Sidhwani under Excel Entertainment. The ensemble cast features Hrithik Roshan, Abhay Deol, Farhan Akhtar, Katrina Kaif, and Kalki Koechlin. The story follows three childhood friends—Arjun, Kabir, and Imran—who reunite for a three-week road trip in Spain that becomes a journey of adventure, reckoning, and growth.

The film was shot across Spain, India, and the UK on an estimated budget of ₹45 crore. Shankar–Ehsaan–Loy composed the music with lyrics by Javed Akhtar. Initially slated for May 2011, the release moved to July 15, 2011. It opened on ~1,800 screens worldwide, became a commercial success (grossing about ₹1.53 billion), and received critical acclaim for direction, writing, music, humor, cinematography, and the cast’s performances.

Plot

Kabir proposes to Natasha and, at their engagement party, she learns he plans a three-week bachelor road trip to Spain with high-school friends Imran and Arjun. Each friend must choose one adventure sport for the group. Arjun—a work-obsessed stockbroker whose career cost him his engagement to Rohini—is reluctant. Imran intends to use the trip to find his biological father, the artist Salman Habib, in Spain.

In Costa Brava the trio meet Laila, a British-Indian fashion design student and diving instructor. Kabir’s pick is deep-sea diving; Laila helps the aquaphobic Arjun overcome his fear, and they grow close. They celebrate La Tomatina in Buñol with Laila and her friend Nuria, and Natasha unexpectedly crashes the trip, suspicious of Kabir.

Arjun’s choice is skydiving in Seville, forcing Imran to face his fear of heights. After a bar brawl lands them briefly in jail, Salman bails them out and gives Imran a candid account of why he left Imran’s mother. Imran recognizes his own mistakes—particularly with Arjun—and apologizes sincerely; their friendship heals. Kabir admits he isn’t ready for marriage.

In Pamplona, they run with the bulls. The three make a pact: Imran will publish his poetry, Arjun will choose life with Laila, and Kabir will be honest with Natasha. As the credits roll, Arjun and Laila marry in Morocco, Kabir and Natasha part amicably, and the friends are renewed.

Production

Development

In November 2009, Zoya Akhtar and Reema Kagti completed a script initially titled Running with the Bulls, later finalized as Zindagi Na Milegi Dobara. Written around the theme of “three guys on the verge of commitments,” the part of Imran was conceived for Farhan Akhtar, who also wrote the dialogues. Poetry by Javed Akhtar is used as voice-over to deepen character interiority. Spain was selected over Mexico to authentically stage the San Fermín bull run climax.

Casting

Farhan Akhtar was the first actor cast, followed by Hrithik Roshan and Abhay Deol. Katrina Kaif was chosen as Laila for her fit with a half-Indian, half-Caucasian, water-confident character; Kalki Koechlin joined as Natasha. Spanish actor Ariadna Cabrol played Nuria.

Filming

Principal photography took place in the UK, Egypt, Mumbai, and in Spain—Barcelona, Pamplona, Buñol, and Andalusia. Cinematographer Carlos Catalan emphasized a warm, realistic look. The La Tomatina sequence required ~16 tons of tomatoes imported from Portugal; “Señorita” was filmed in Alájar with local participation. The bull-run finale was shot in Pamplona; final schedules concluded around December 2010.

Soundtrack

Composers Shankar–Ehsaan–Loy collaborated with lyricist Javed Akhtar. Flamenco singer María del Mar Fernández performed on “Señorita,” alongside the singing debuts of Roshan and Deol; popular tracks also include “Ik Junoon.”

Marketing

Marketing involved mobile partnerships, trailers, brand tie-ins (Mountain Dew, Gillette, ING Vysya), and a road-trip promotion from Mumbai to Delhi culminating in a concert. A mobile game based on La Tomatina accompanied the campaign.

Release

Originally slated for May 27, 2011, the film was delayed to June 24 and then July 15, ultimately releasing on ~1,800 screens. A special screening at Shah Rukh Khan’s residence followed the premiere; some proceeds supported victims of the 2011 Mumbai bombings. Home media arrived August 30, 2011; later, the film streamed on Netflix and Amazon Prime Video.

Critical reception

Critics widely praised direction, writing, music, humor, cinematography, and performances. Reviewers such as Rajeev Masand, Mayank Shekhar, Anna M. M. Vetticad, and others gave positive notices; some dissent (e.g., Raja Sen) cited tonal coolness and length. Rotten Tomatoes reports a strong positive ratio among critiques (values vary by snapshot).

“Takes the tone of a fun, all-boys road trip to give you a heartfelt message on seizing the moment.” — Rajeev Masand

Box office

Opening second to Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 2, the film performed strongly in multiplexes, sustained a multi-week run alongside Singham, and ranked among the year’s highest domestic and overseas earners. Estimated worldwide gross: ₹1.53 billion.

Controversies

PETA criticized the bull-running depiction; producers cited clearances and care for animal welfare. Spanish-American artist Charo petitioned the director to remove bull-run scenes.

Potential sequel

Talk of a sequel has surfaced periodically; creators and cast have expressed both interest and reservations over the years. As of now, no official sequel is released.
